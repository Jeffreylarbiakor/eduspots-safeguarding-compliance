01

What this tool is

A network-wide view of who has completed safeguarding training, when their training expires, and who needs to renew — across all 130+ Catalysts and headteachers in the EduSpots network.

Right now, training compliance across 130+ Catalysts likely lives in a patchwork of spreadsheets, WhatsApp conversations with RCs, and calendar reminders held by individuals. That works when the network is small. It starts to break down when you need to answer questions like: which Catalysts haven't renewed in over a year? How many of Cynthia's Spots have an overdue training in this cycle? Is the Northern cluster's compliance rate lower than the Volta cluster's, and if so, why?

This tracker consolidates that picture. It doesn't replace the RC relationships or the Keeping Spots Safe policy — it sits above them, giving the Head of Programme and Product Operations a network-level view of training compliance that makes renewal follow-ups faster, more consistent, and easier to account for when reporting to trustees or funders.

Network-wide in one view
See all 130+ Catalysts and their training status without opening a single spreadsheet or chasing an RC for an update.
Specific, date-based flags
Every flag carries the exact reason — "Training expired 47 days ago" not just a red dot — so you know what conversation to have before you pick up the phone.
30-day early warning
Catalysts flagged as "expiring soon" before they tip into overdue — so renewal conversations happen with lead time, not after the fact.
Human decision, always
The tool surfaces who needs follow-up. Every renewal conversation, RC notification, and training arrangement is made by a person — the Head of Ops, an RC, or both together.

02

What it doesn't do — and why

This is a training logistics tracker. Getting the scope right is not a design preference — it's a safeguarding requirement.

The most important thing to understand about this tool is what it was deliberately designed not to do. A safeguarding compliance tracker that also stored incident reports, concerns, or case notes would be dangerous: it would mix training logistics with sensitive personal data in a system not designed to handle it, and would risk creating an unofficial parallel record outside EduSpots' actual safeguarding processes.

Structural scope boundary

Tracked in this tool

  • Training completion dates
  • Training expiry dates (12-month cycle)
  • Renewal status per Catalyst
  • Compliance rate by RC cluster
  • Training completion history (dates only)
  • Who is overdue and by how many days
  • Who is due to expire in the next 30 days
  • RC acknowledgement of follow-up notifications

Not in this tool — by design

  • Safeguarding incidents or reports
  • Concerns raised about any individual
  • Allegations or case notes
  • Catalyst conduct information of any kind
  • Contact details for children or families
  • Risk assessments or investigations
  • Any field that could be misread as a conduct record

This boundary is structural, not just a display choice. The data model has no fields for incident or concern data — they cannot be added to a row in the database. They are not placeholders for a future version. For actual safeguarding concerns, follow EduSpots' Keeping Spots Safe policy and contact the designated leads directly.

03

The core principle

Automation flags dates. A human decides what to do about them.

🌿
Why this matters for safeguarding specifically

A tool that auto-escalated overdue Catalysts to a central register, or that generated reports about an individual's training history without human review, would introduce risk rather than reduce it. An RC knows that a Catalyst is mid-way through a rescheduled training block, that a Spot has been temporarily closed, that a headteacher has been dealing with a family emergency. The tracker doesn't have that context. So it flags; you decide.

This principle is built into every interactive element. The "Acknowledge — reminder sent" button on a flagged Catalyst doesn't change that Catalyst's compliance status, mark them as retrained, or close the flag. It records that a human has notified the relevant RC and that follow-up ownership has been logged. Status can only change when a real completed training event is recorded in the data — nothing the tool does can shortcut that.

04

Overview view

The starting screen. A pulse-check on the whole network before you go deeper.

Overview
KPI row · compliance-by-cluster chart · 12-month completions chart

The Overview loads first when you open the tracker. It answers the most common Monday-morning question: what's the state of training compliance across the network right now?

The KPI row gives five numbers at a glance. The two charts underneath show whether renewal activity is keeping pace with expiries, and whether any RC cluster is systematically falling behind.

  • Total Catalysts The full count of Catalysts in the tracker. In a live system, this would match the active Catalyst register maintained by EduSpots — not including volunteers who have left the network.
  • Currently compliant The percentage of all Catalysts with a valid, non-expired training record. This is the headline compliance figure — the one to track week-on-week and report to trustees. A rising percentage means renewal follow-ups are working; a falling one means the network is getting behind.
  • Expiring in 30 days Catalysts whose current training expires within the next 30 days. These are the early-warning cases — they're still technically compliant, but need to schedule a renewal now to avoid slipping into overdue. This is the number to act on before it becomes a problem.
  • Currently overdue Catalysts whose training has already expired. Any Catalyst in this count is operating without a valid training record. A high number here warrants an urgent conversation with the relevant RC cluster leads.
  • Average days overdue For overdue Catalysts only — how long, on average, training has been lapsed. A low number suggests recent expiry (renewals are just slipping behind); a high number suggests a systemic gap in a cluster or Spot where follow-up hasn't been reaching.
  • Compliance rate by RC cluster A horizontal bar chart showing each RC's cluster compliance rate. Green bars indicate 85%+ compliance; gold indicates 70–84%; clay/red below 70%. An obvious imbalance here is a prompt for a direct conversation with that RC about what's causing the gap — not an automatic escalation.
  • Training completions — last 12 months A line chart showing how many training completions were recorded each calendar month. Lets you spot seasonal patterns (e.g. training surges before school terms), identify months where completions dropped unexpectedly, and check whether renewal activity is keeping pace with the network's 12-month cycle.

05

Compliance roster

Every Catalyst in the network, their current status, and their training history — in one filterable table.

Compliance roster
All Catalysts · filterable by RC cluster and status · click for training history

The roster is the operational record of the network's training compliance. Every Catalyst appears here with their Spot, RC, completion date, expiry date, and status. Filter to narrow to a specific cluster before an RC check-in call, or filter to "Overdue" to get an immediate working list for urgent follow-up.

Clicking any row opens a detail panel showing that Catalyst's full training history — a simple list of completion dates in reverse chronological order, with the most recent marked "current". Nothing beyond training dates is stored or shown.

  • Name The Catalyst's name. In a live system this would link to the master Catalyst register. Note: in this prototype, all names are synthetic composites — no real individuals are represented.
  • Spot The named EduSpots location this Catalyst is associated with. All Spot names in the prototype are real — drawn from EduSpots' published network of 49+ Spots across Ghana.
  • Regional Coordinator The RC whose cluster this Spot belongs to: Cynthia Mawuena Tetteh (Volta Region), Getrude Akunlibe (Northern Region), Abdul Wadud Suleiman (Central/Western Regions), or Abdul-Malik Iddrisu (New Spots).
  • Completion date The date the most recent training was completed. The 12-month renewal window runs from this date.
  • Expiry date Completion date + 12 months. If today is past this date, the Catalyst is overdue. If today is within 30 days of this date, the Catalyst is expiring soon.
  • Status badge Computed automatically from the expiry date — never manually assigned. Green = compliant, gold = expiring soon, clay/red = overdue. The badge colour matches the same palette used across all five portfolio tools.
  • RC cluster filter Narrows the table to a single RC's Catalysts. Use this before a cluster check-in call — you'll arrive knowing exactly which of that RC's Catalysts are flagged before the conversation starts.
  • Status filter Filter to "Overdue" for an immediate working list of Catalysts who need urgent follow-up. Filter to "Expiring soon" to plan ahead. Filter to "Compliant" to confirm which Spots are covered.
  • Training history (detail panel) Click any row to see the full list of prior training completions for that Catalyst — dates only, in reverse chronological order. This is the complete scope of the detail view. There are no conduct fields, no notes fields, and no free-text input in this panel.

06

RC clusters

Compliance grouped by Regional Coordinator — the fastest way to identify which cluster needs the most attention this cycle.

RC clusters
4 clusters · compliant / expiring / overdue counts · cluster compliance %

Each row in this view represents one Regional Coordinator and the Catalysts within their cluster. This is the view to open before a cluster-level reporting conversation — with the Keeping Spots Safe Coordinator, the Head of Education, or in a trustee update.

The cluster compliance percentage is colour-coded: green at 85%+, gold between 70–84%, clay below 70%. An obvious gap between clusters is a signal, not a conclusion — it warrants a conversation with that RC about what's driving the numbers, not an automatic escalation.

  • Total Total number of Catalysts in this RC's cluster.
  • Compliant Catalysts with a valid, non-expired training record in this cluster.
  • Expiring soon Catalysts in this cluster whose training expires within 30 days. The gold badge signals early-warning status — still compliant, but needs scheduling now.
  • Overdue Catalysts in this cluster whose training has already expired. The clay/red badge signals that follow-up with the RC is needed.
  • Cluster % The percentage of this RC's Catalysts who are currently compliant. The colour-coded pill (green / gold / clay) gives an at-a-glance read of cluster health without needing to calculate from the raw counts.
💡
How to use this view in a cluster reporting conversation

Pull this view up before a call or written update. If one cluster's percentage is significantly lower than the others, it becomes the lead agenda item — which Spots are contributing most to the gap, and what does the RC need from HQ to close it. The Compliance Roster filtered by that RC's name gives the specific Catalyst-level detail for the conversation.

07

Renewal reminders

The action view — every Catalyst who needs follow-up, with the specific reason and a way to log that the relevant RC has been notified.

Renewal reminders
Flagged Catalysts only · specific date-based reasons · acknowledge for follow-up

This view collects every Catalyst who is overdue or expiring soon — sorted with the worst cases at the top (most overdue first, then by how soon expiry is approaching). Each card shows the specific reason in plain language, so you arrive at a follow-up conversation already knowing what you're dealing with.

The "Acknowledge — reminder sent" button records that the relevant RC has been notified and owns follow-up. It doesn't raise a training record, doesn't change the Catalyst's status, and doesn't send a notification automatically. It's a human handshake: I've seen this, the RC has been contacted, follow-up is in hand.

  • Date-based reason Every card states the specific, date-derived reason the Catalyst was flagged — e.g. "Training expired 23 days ago" or "Expires in 11 days — renewal not yet scheduled." Never a behavioural inference, always a date calculation.
  • Spot and RC on the card The Catalyst's Spot name and RC are shown directly on each card — you don't need to open a detail panel to know who to contact about follow-up.
  • Expiry date The specific date training expired or will expire — shown alongside the reason so you have the exact date if you need it for a written update or RC conversation.
  • Acknowledge — reminder sent Marks that the RC has been notified and follow-up ownership has been logged. The button becomes a "✓ Reminder sent to RC" confirmation and the card dims. In this prototype, acknowledgements reset on page refresh — in a live system they would persist with a timestamp and the name of whoever logged them.
  • Sort order Overdue Catalysts (most lapsed first) appear above expiring-soon Catalysts — so the most urgent cases are at the top of the list without any manual sorting needed.
💡
How to use this view in a weekly safeguarding review

Open this view at the start of each week. Work through the overdue cards first. For each: is this situation already known to the relevant RC? Acknowledge it. Is it a surprise? It needs a direct call. Then move to expiring-soon — these are the early warnings to address before they become overdue. The goal is to reduce the number of cards in this view over time, not to manage a permanent list of flags.

08

How status is calculated

Every status is computed by a named function with explicit rules — no hidden scoring, no black box, no behavioural inference.

The rules are the same regardless of which view you're in. A Catalyst flagged as "overdue" in the Renewal Reminders view is overdue for the same reason their badge shows clay/red in the Compliance Roster. The reason is always shown explicitly alongside the status — never implied by a colour alone.

Training compliance status

Overdue
Today's date is past the Catalyst's expiry date.
The tool calculates: today > completionDate + 12 months. The exact number of days lapsed is shown on the Renewal Reminders card: "Training expired X days ago."
Expiring soon
The expiry date is within the next 30 days (and not already past).
The tool calculates: 0 ≤ daysUntilExpiry ≤ 30. The exact countdown is shown: "Expires in X days — renewal not yet scheduled."
Compliant
Neither of the above conditions apply.
The expiry date is more than 30 days away. The Catalyst has a valid, current training record. No flag is raised.
📋
The 30-day window and 12-month cycle can be adjusted

Both the 30-day expiring-soon threshold and the 12-month renewal cycle are starting points, not fixed policy. In a live deployment, the Head of Ops, the Keeping Spots Safe Coordinator, and the Head of Education would review these together and set thresholds that reflect what EduSpots considers a healthy training cadence. The renewal cycle assumption in particular should be confirmed against the actual Keeping Spots Safe programme specification before this tracker goes live.

🔒
What status can and cannot represent

Status reflects only one thing: whether a training record is current, approaching expiry, or lapsed. It cannot and does not reflect anything about a Catalyst's conduct, reliability, or safeguarding record. A Catalyst who is "overdue" has a lapsed training date — the tool makes no other inference from that fact.

09

Typical weekly workflow

How the Head of Programme and Product Operations would use this in practice — from Monday morning to a follow-up conversation with an RC.

1
Open Overview — read the network pulse
Check the KPI row. Has overall compliance gone up or down since last week? Has the overdue count grown? Has the average days-overdue figure climbed, suggesting a cluster is falling further behind rather than catching up? These numbers set the agenda for the rest of the session.
2
Open Renewal Reminders — work through the overdue list
Sort is automatic — worst cases first. For each overdue card: is this situation already known and being handled by the RC? Acknowledge it. Is it a surprise? That's a direct call to the RC. Move through expiring-soon cards next — these are the ones to act on before the next weekly review, not after.
3
Prepare for RC cluster calls — check the Clusters view
Before a check-in call with, say, Getrude Akunlibe (Northern Region), open the Clusters view to see her cluster's overall compliance rate relative to the others. Then go to the Compliance Roster, filter by her name, and sort by status to see exactly which of her Catalysts are flagged. Arrive at the call with a specific agenda — not a general "how are things going?"
4
Use the Roster for funder or trustee reporting
Filter to "All statuses" and the full network count gives the headline compliance figure for a written report. Filter to "Overdue" for the number of lapsed records. The cluster view gives a per-RC breakdown. These numbers can be pulled directly into a board or funder update without reformatting.
5
Make the renewal decision — outside the tool
The tracker tells you who needs to train and when. The actual arrangement — scheduling a training session, coordinating with the Keeping Spots Safe Coordinator or Head of Education, reaching out to a Catalyst directly — happens outside the tool, in conversation with the relevant RC. The tracker is the shared evidence base, not the decision-maker.

Who does what

The tracker serves three roles differently. The tool does the date-tracking; people make every decision that matters.

Task Who In the tool
Monitor network-wide training compliance Head of Ops Overview KPIs + Clusters view
Identify which Catalysts are overdue for renewal Head of Ops Renewal Reminders — overdue cards
Prepare for cluster check-in calls Head of Ops RC Clusters view + Roster filtered by RC
Log that an RC has been notified of a lapsed record Head of Ops RC Acknowledge button — Renewal Reminders
Review training history for a specific Catalyst Head of Ops RC Catalyst detail modal — Compliance Roster
Arrange or schedule a training renewal Head of Ops RC Outside the tool — informed by it
Report compliance figures to funders or trustees Head of Ops Overview KPIs · Clusters view · Roster count
Liaise with Keeping Spots Safe Coordinator Head of Ops Outside the tool — tracker provides the data basis
Handle actual safeguarding concerns or incidents Head of Ops RC Outside the tool — follow Keeping Spots Safe policy
Compute training status and flag renewals Automated computeComplianceStatus() in data.js

11

Next steps if built for real

This is a working prototype built on synthetic data. Here's what a live deployment would add.

Live training records connection
Connect to a shared Google Sheet or Airtable base that RCs already update after each training session — so the tracker reflects real records without requiring a new data entry habit.
WhatsApp / email digest
A weekly summary sent to the Head of Ops and each RC listing new flags in their cluster — so the tracker pushes renewal reminders rather than waiting to be opened.
CSV export for reporting
Export the full roster or the overdue list to CSV for inclusion in trustee packs, funder reports, or RC cluster updates — without reformatting anything manually.
RC-level authentication
Each RC logs in to see only their own cluster's Catalysts — protecting personal information and ensuring RCs have a focused view of their own responsibilities rather than the full network.
Acknowledge with attribution
Persist acknowledgements with a timestamp and the name of the person who logged them — a clear audit trail of who took ownership of a flagged Catalyst's follow-up and when.
Integration with Project #1
Cross-reference training compliance with Spot activity data from the MEL Dashboard — so a Spot with low engagement AND an overdue Catalyst flags as a combined priority, not two separate items to track in separate tools.
